Advanced Diffusion MRI Technique Development I: Spiral Based Techniques

Diffusion MRI plays a very important role in both clinical diagnosis and neuroscience research. Single-shot EPI DWI suffers from the problems of low-resolution, geometric distortion and image blurring. My laboratory has been systematically developing navigator-free, navigated and self-navigated multi-shot DWI methods based on either EPI or spiral sampling trajectories.

High Resolution Diffusion MRI using Navigator-Free Regular Spiral:

A new proposed reconstruction method, named POCS-enhanced Inherent Correction of motion-induced phase Errors (POCS-ICE), can reliably reconstruct multi-shot spiral DWI images without use of navigator, for even relatively high shot number. In vivo experiments show the improved image quality in terms of higher SNR and less artifacts compared with SENSE+CG. The improved spatial resolution and image resolvability are beneficial to study brain micro-structures and physiological features for neuroscience.
